ISO INTEGRATED MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS

  • Duration: 10 days
  • CPD Points: 5
  • Cost: R10 000

Prerequisite

Participants should have a basic understanding of management principles and systems in quality, environmental, and occupational health & safety to fully benefit from this integrated course.

Outline

This extensive course integrates key elements from ISO standards related to quality, environmental, and occupational health & safety management systems:

Quality Management Systems (ISO 9001:2015):

This standard provides a comprehensive approach to quality management, covering quality planning, risk management, and performance evaluation to ensure continual improvement of a Quality Management System (QMS).

Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems (ISO 45001:2018):

This standard focuses on establishing, maintaining, and improving an Occupational Health and Safety Management System (OHSMS). It includes guidelines for hazard identification, risk assessment, and compliance with legal requirements.

Occupational Health and Safety Guidelines (ISO 45002:2022):

Offering practical implementation advice for ISO 45001, this standard emphasizes stakeholder engagement and documentation requirements.

Health and Safety During COVID-19 (ISO 45005:2020):

This guideline addresses the management of health and safety in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic, including risk assessment, preventive measures, and response planning.

Risk Management (ISO 31000:2018):

Provides principles and a framework for managing risk, encompassing risk assessment techniques and strategies for risk treatment.

Occupational Health Management (ISO 45006:2021):

Focuses on integrating occupational health into the OHSMS, including health surveillance and health management strategies.

Environmental Management Systems (ISO 14001:2015):

This standard offers a framework for establishing, maintaining, and improving an Environmental Management System (EMS), with a focus on setting environmental objectives, conducting audits, and ensuring compliance.

Environmental Management Implementation Guidelines (ISO 14004:2016):

Provides practical steps for implementing ISO 14001, including stakeholder engagement and documentation practices

Phased Implementation of EMS (ISO 14005:2020):

Details strategies for the gradual implementation of an EMS, setting incremental goals, and managing organizational change.

Ecodesign Principles (ISO 14006:2020):

Guides the incorporation of ecodesign principles into the EMS, aimed at enhancing product sustainability and reducing environmental impacts.

Greenhouse Gas Reporting (ISO 14064-1:2018):

Outlines methods for quantifying and reporting greenhouse gas emissions and removals, including the creation of GHG inventories and impact assessments.

Benefits

Participants will gain a thorough understanding of quality, environmental, and occupational health & safety management systems. They will learn to effectively implement ISO 9001 for quality management, ISO 14001 for environmental management, and ISO 45001 for occupational health and safety. The course also covers phased EMS implementation, ecodesign for improved product sustainability, and GHG reporting. This integrated approach will enhance their skills in system integration, risk management, and regulatory compliance, supporting their professional growth with CPD points and certification.

Assessment

Participants will sit a 3-hour assessment at an invigilated venue at the end of the course. A minimum pass mark of 60% is required.

Appropriate For

Quality managers, environmental managers, health and safety professionals, compliance officers, and sustainability consultants involved in implementing and managing integrated management systems.
