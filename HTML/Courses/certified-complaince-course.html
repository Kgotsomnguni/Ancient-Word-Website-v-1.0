CERTIFIED COMPLIANCE OFFICER (CCO)

  • Duration: 1 month
  • CPD Points: 5
  • Cost: R30 000

Prerequisite

Participants should have a matriculation certificate, be in a supervisory or higher role, and possess at least three years of relevant experience. They must have knowledge of any listed fields or be a compliance officer.

Outline

This course covers essential aspects of workplace management and compliance, combining various standards, guidelines, and practices:

Quality Management System (QMS)

Quality Planning:

: Developing a systematic approach to define quality objectives and strategies. This includes setting quality standards, creating detailed plans to achieve these standards, and ensuring that processes are in place to meet customer requirements.

Risk Management:

Identifying potential risks that could impact product or service quality. This involves assessing the likelihood and impact of these risks, implementing measures to mitigate them, and monitoring the effectiveness of these measures.

Performance Evaluation:

: Continuously measuring and analyzing the performance of the QMS. This includes using key performance indicators (KPIs) to assess how well quality objectives are being met, conducting audits, and reviewing customer feedback to drive continuous improvement.

Occupational Health and Safety

Hazard Identification:

: Systematic process for recognizing potential hazards in the workplace. This includes physical, chemical, biological, and ergonomic hazards that could pose risks to employee health and safety.

Risk Assessment:

: Evaluating the risks associated with identified hazards to determine their potential impact and likelihood. This involves prioritizing risks based on their severity and implementing control measures to manage them.

Emergency Preparedness:

Developing and implementing plans and procedures for responding to emergencies such as fires, chemical spills, or medical incidents. This includes conducting drills, ensuring effective communication, and providing training to employees.

Occupational Hygiene

Hazard Identification and Evaluation:

Techniques for detecting and assessing chemical, physical, and biological hazards in the workplace. This includes using monitoring equipment, conducting surveys, and analyzing exposure data.

Control Measures:

Implementing strategies to control or eliminate identified risks. This may involve engineering controls (e.g., ventilation systems), administrative controls (e.g., work rotation), and personal protective equipment (PPE).

Health Surveillance Programs:

: Monitoring and evaluating workers' health to detect any adverse effects from occupational exposures. This includes regular health checks, biological monitoring, and maintaining records to track health trends.

Environmental Management

Waste Management:

Strategies for reducing, reusing, and recycling waste materials. This involves implementing waste minimization practices, ensuring proper disposal methods, and adhering to regulatory requirements.

Resource Conservation:

: Techniques for optimizing the use of natural resources such as energy, water, and raw materials. This includes implementing energy-efficient practices, reducing resource consumption, and promoting sustainable practices.

Sustainability Practices:

Developing and integrating policies to support environmental sustainability. This involves setting sustainability goals, engaging with stakeholders, and continuously improving practices to minimize environmental impact.

Labour Law

Workers' Rights:

Understanding and upholding the legal rights of employees, including fair treatment, safe working conditions, and protection against unfair dismissal.

Anti-Discrimination Laws:

Laws and regulations preventing discrimination based on race, gender, age, disability, and other characteristics. This includes ensuring equal opportunities and fair treatment in hiring, promotions, and employment practices.

Compensation and Benefits:

Regulations concerning employee compensation, benefits, and working conditions. This includes understanding wage laws, overtime regulations, and employee benefit entitlements.

Handling Legal Disputes:

Approaches for managing and resolving legal disputes, grievances, and complaints in the workplace. This includes understanding dispute resolution mechanisms and ensuring compliance with legal procedures.

Ergonomics

Workstation Design:

Creating ergonomic workstations to improve comfort and reduce physical strain. This includes adjusting desk height, chair design, and equipment placement to fit individual needs.

Ergonomic Assessments:

: Conducting assessments to identify ergonomic risks and issues in the workplace. This involves analyzing work processes, equipment use, and employee posture to recommend improvements.

Reducing Musculoskeletal Disorders:

: Implementing measures to prevent and manage musculoskeletal disorders. This includes ergonomic training, promoting proper lifting techniques, and designing tasks to reduce repetitive strain and discomfort.

RISK Management

Risk Identification:

Systematic approach to identifying risks that could impact organizational objectives. This includes assessing internal and external factors that could pose threats to operations, projects, or strategic goals.

Risk Assessment:

Analyzing the potential impact and likelihood of identified risks. This involves evaluating the severity of each risk and prioritizing them based on their potential effects on the organization.

Risk Mitigation

Developing and implementing strategies to manage and mitigate identified risks. This includes creating action plans to reduce risk likelihood and impact, and allocating resources to address risks effectively.

Risk Monitoring and Review:

Continuously monitoring risks and reviewing the effectiveness of risk management strategies. This involves regular updates to risk assessments, tracking risk indicators, and adapting mitigation plans as necessary.

Assessment

Participants will undertake a 3-hour exam at an invigilated venue at the end of the course. A minimum pass mark of 60% is required to achieve certification.

Appropriate For

Health and safety managers, quality managers, compliance officers, environmental managers, occupational health professionals, ergonomists, and consultants involved in managing and improving workplace systems and practices.
